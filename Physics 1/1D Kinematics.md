## Vectors
When we draw a vector, its length represents its magnitude and the arrow points to its direction.
![[Pasted image 20240305193023.png]]
- Two vectors are *identical* if they have the same *magnitude* and *direction*.
- A vector is the *negative* of another if they have the same direction but oriented $180 \degree$ from it
### Addition of Vectors
#### Graphical Method
Simply add two vectors end to end and draw the new vector.
![[Pasted image 20240305193347.png]]
Also, when subtracting two vectors you can think that as the addition of the negative vector.
![[Pasted image 20240305193550.png]]
#### Method by Components
In this method you separate the vectors into it's components and add those components to each other to get the resultant vector.
![[Pasted image 20240305193735.png]]
Some formulas you need to know about this:
- $\vec{A_x} = \vec{A} \cdot cos(\theta)$
- $\vec{A_y} = \vec{A} \cdot sin(\theta)$
- $tan^{-1}(\frac{A_y}{A_x}) = \theta$
- $A = \sqrt{A^2_x + A^2_y}$
### Multiplication of Vector by Scalar
- The magnitude of the vector will be equal to its initial magnitude multiplied by the scalar.
- The direction will be the same if the scalar is positive, or it will be opposite if the scalar is negative.
- The resultant is always a vector.
## Displacement vs Distance Traveled
![[Pasted image 20240305194448.png]]
- Displacement: $30m$
- Distance Traveled: $110m$
Displacement is a vector but Distance Traveled is a scalar value.
### Speed
$$
average\ speed = \frac{distance\ traveled}{time\ elapsed}
$$
Speed is a scalar value.
### Velocity
$$
average\ velocity = \frac{displacement}{time\ elapsed}
$$
Because velocity is a product of displacement (which is a vector), it is also a vector.
$$
\vec{v} = \frac{x_2 - x_1}{t_2 - t_1} = \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}
$$
#### Instantaneous Velocity
$$
v = \lim_{\Delta t \to 0}\big(\frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}\big) = \frac{dx}{dt}
$$
![[Pasted image 20240305195502.png]]
- If the slope of the curve is positive, the velocity is positive.
- If the slope is zero, the velocity is zero.
- If the slope is negative, the velocity is negative.
### Acceleration
$$
average\ acceleration = \frac{change\ of\ velocity}{time\ elapsed}
$$
$$
a = \frac{v_2 - v_1}{t_2 - t_1} = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}
$$
#### Instantaneous Acceleration
$$
a = \lim_{\Delta t \to 0}\big(\frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}\big) = \frac{dv}{dt}
$$
