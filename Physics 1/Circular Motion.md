## Key Concepts
- If the object has a *constant magnitude of velocity* while it performs circular motion, we call this motion **uniform circular motion**.
- In *uniform circular motion*, despite the magnitude of the velocity remaining constant, the continuous change in direction results for an **acceleration**.
	- We call this acceleration **centripetal acceleration**.
- In *circular motion* velocity and acceleration vectors are *always perpendicular*.
## Radians
One radian is the measure of the central angle whose *arc length* is the same as the *radius* of the circle.

## Formulas
### Variables
- $r \to$ Radius
- $f \to$ Frequency                            $T \to$ Period
- $a \to$ Tangential Acceleration      $\alpha \to$ Angular Acceleration
- $v \to$ Tangential Velocity              $\omega \to$ Angular Velocity
### Formulas
$$
v = \omega r
$$
$$
a = \alpha r
$$
**NOTE:** To convert *tangential* velocity or acceleration to it's *angular* all you need to do is multiplying it with $r$.
$$
a = \frac{v^2}{r} = \omega^2r
$$
$$
T = \frac{1}{f} = \frac{2 \pi}{\omega}
$$
