## Line Segments
Let $A$ and $B$ be points in a space:
- If $A \neq B \to$ There exists a unique line $r$ through $A$ and $B$ called *line segment* bounded by $A$ and $B$.
- If $A = B \to$ The line described above is not determined anymore. However we can still talk about a *line segment* bounded by $A$ and $B$. That would be the unique point $A = B$. We call this *degenerate segment* or *null segment*.
A line segment is denoted by $\overline{AB}$
